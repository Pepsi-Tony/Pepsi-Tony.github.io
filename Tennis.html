Tennis

The predecessor of tennis was a game called "handball" that was popular in the French court in the 14th century. The rule is that two players, separated by a rope, use their palms to hit each other with a ball made of cloth wrapped in hair. Afterwards, the game was developed and improved, and the rope was replaced by a net, and the hand hitting was changed to a wooden racket, and then the wooden racket was pulled on the string. Beginning in the 18th century, this game also began to appear among European folks, and it became popular in Europe in the 19th century. Modern tennis was invented in England by Major Walter Clopton Wingfield in December 1873 as a game for entertaining guests at backyard parties. He created the game based on the old game of indoor tennis or proto-tennis. According to most tennis historians, most of the rules of modern tennis were borrowed by Winfield from handball, indoor tennis, and French royal tennis.





Novak Djokovic


He is a Serbian professional tennis player. He has been ranked No. 1 in the world for 373 consecutive weeks and has been No. 1 at the end of the year seven times. He has won 21 Grand Slam men's singles titles, including a record nine Australian Open titles. He is currently ranked fifth in the world in singles by the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP). Overall, he has won 92 ATP singles titles, including a record 65 Grand Slam titles, a joint record six year-end championships and a record 38 Masters titles. Djokovic completed a non-calendar-year Grand Slam in singles, becoming the only player in tennis history to be the four-time Grand Slam champion on three different surfaces at the same time. He is also the first person in the Open Era to achieve a double career Grand Slam in singles and the only player to complete a Career Gold Masters in singles on the ATP Tour, which he has done twice.















Li na


She is a Chinese former professional tennis player.She achieved a career-high WTA ranking of world No. 2 on 17 February 2014. Over the course of her career, Li won nine WTA Tour singles titles including two Grand Slam singles titles at the 2011 French Open and 2014 Australian Open. Those victories made her the first Grand Slam singles champion from Asia, male or female. She also became the first player representing an Asian country to appear in a Grand Slam singles final, finishing as the runner-up at the 2011 Australian Open. Li was also the runner-up at the 2013 Australian Open and 2013 WTA Tour Championships, a three-time quarterfinalist at Wimbledon and a semifinalist at the 2008 Beijing Olympic Games and 2013 US Open. Among her other most notable achievements, she was the first Chinese player to win a WTA tour title at the Guangzhou International Women's Open in 2004, the first to reach a Grand Slam singles quarterfinal at the 2006 Wimbledon Championships, and the first to break into the world's top ten.
